Gems.

Six small apps, one premise: the people who actually visited a place already wrote the guidebook. We just had to read all of it and refuse to summarise.

Editor’s note

Over one weekend we ingested tens of thousands of reviews, transcripts, and forum threads about the Louvre and the Colosseum — from TripAdvisor, Google, Reddit, and YouTube — and ran them through a clustering and labelling pipeline that produces structured, citable claims instead of vibe-y summaries.

Each app below is a different way of reading that signal: as a verdict, as a route, as a warning sheet, as a list of things our catalog doesn’t sell tickets to yet. Every claim is footnoted to the person who said it. Nothing here is generic AI fluff — if you click through to a quote you’ll find a real reviewer on a real date.

Scope: Louvre Museum and the Colosseum. Two POIs, deep coverage, no padding.
